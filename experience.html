Purpose for my Website

My name is Taylor Gallop, I am a teacher in Chicago Public Schools and have been teaching Third Grade for three years now. I work at the Alfred Nobel Dual Language School and have learned so much valuable information through my short time as a teacher that I am excited to share with any other new or experienced teachers!

  1. Share my personal experiences as a teacher in CPS.
  2. Exchange information, including project and anchor chart ideas.
  3. Document my career and the progress that I make as a teacher.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in the belief that every student has unique strengths and potential that can be nurtured through a supportive and engaging learning environment. I strive to create a classroom where curiosity is encouraged, and where students feel safe to take risks and express their ideas. I believe in employing a variety of instructional strategies to accommodate diverse learning styles and needs, ensuring that each student can connect with the material in a meaningful way. Building strong, trusting relationships with my students is at the core of my approach, as I see education not just as a transfer of knowledge but as a collaborative journey. By fostering a love for learning and promoting critical thinking, I aim to empower my students to become confident, independent learners who are prepared to face the challenges of the future.

Professional Experience

As a fourth-year teacher in a third-grade classroom at Chicago Public Schools, I have honed my skills in nurturing both academic and social development in my students. My classroom is a dynamic space where I blend innovative teaching methods with a deep understanding of each student’s unique learning needs. I use differentiated instruction to ensure that every child receives the support they need, tailoring my approach to fit various learning styles. By closely monitoring student progress through data-driven assessments, I adjust my teaching strategies to address individual strengths and areas for improvement. I also make it a priority to collaborate with my colleagues and engage with parents to build a strong, supportive community around our students. My passion for teaching and commitment to my students' success have been key in creating an enriching learning environment where every child can thrive.

Growth as an Educator

Over the course of my teaching career, I have learned invaluable lessons and made significant strides in my professional development. I have come to appreciate the importance of flexibility and adaptability in the classroom, recognizing that each day brings new challenges and opportunities for growth. Through ongoing reflection and feedback, I have refined my instructional techniques, learning to tailor my approach to meet the diverse needs of my students more effectively. Embracing technology and innovative teaching methods has allowed me to engage my students in new and exciting ways, making learning more interactive and impactful. My ability to build strong relationships with students and their families has deepened, creating a more supportive and collaborative classroom environment. Overall, the progress I’ve made as an educator has not only enhanced my teaching practice but also significantly contributed to my students' academic and personal growth.
